Week 6 Recap

START SPREADING THE NEWS, SARREL DOESN’T LOSE!

A championship rematch, top teams panties in a bunch early, Sarrel finally entering the win column, and Wagner surviving an absolute heavyweight fight.

🏆 Championship Rematch

Justin Survives JohnnySack

The championship rematch did not disappoint.

This was a back-and-forth matchup all week, but in the end, Justin defeats JohnnySack 703.37–592.47. Justin moves to 5–1, while JohnnySack slides to 1–5.

JohnnySack looked like he had this matchup IN THE BAG. By Friday, he was sitting at over an 80% win probability and looked like he was cruising.

Then everything flipped.

Justin got absolutely blessed. Max Meyer — a bum with a CAREER 5 ERA — randomly drops 60 points. Then KNOWN BUM Mitch Keller, also sitting around a CAREER 5 ERA, puts up 53 points on Friday.

Of course.

And it did not stop there.

Ranger Suarez went from borderline unplayable to dropping 76 points early in the week, then tacking on another 22-point performance later. He finished with 98 total points and claimed this week’s top scorer position.

And honestly? Seeing Ranger leave Sunday’s game early with an injury? I LOVED IT! Serves him right.

Meanwhile, JohnnySack is just sitting there watching it all happen. Casey Mize is scratched Sunday due to injury, Joe Ryan throws 3 pitches & is removed from the game Sunday, Randy Vasquez is scratced Sunday & slated to start Monday instead. When does it end?

At this point, you have to ask: Who has it worse than Johnny Sack?

Justin was bitching and moaning per usual early in the week, while JohnnySack is now left fuming. And somehow, Justin continued his managerial malpractice by picking up former JohnnySack roster members: Jack, aka Hack Flaherty, who gave him 0 points; Merrill Kelly who gave him -3; and Brandon Sproat who gave him -4.

Despite Justin trying to get cute and pick up former JohnnySack players, he still gets blessed and wins.

JohnnySack had the #3 scorer of the week, Byron Buxton, with 89.7 points, and the #4 scorer, Clay Holmes, with 87.53 points. And he still lost.

He cannot catch a break.

The playoffs are still the goal, but JohnnySack needs to string a few wins together fast. He also needs to figure out what to do with Rafael Devers, currently the 395th ranked player in our league.

Devers has been absolutely brutal. Maybe he put on a few too many in the offseason. Fat bastard.


⚔️ Wagner vs Moltisanti’s Mistress

Wagner Wins a Slugfest

Wagner takes down Moltisanti’s Mistress 817.03-802.37.

Two top-end teams slugged it out all week in a back-and-forth, high-scoring matchup.

Moltisanti’s Mistress came back from a deficit over 165 points, but it still wasn't enough. Despite giving Wagner a Sunday night heart attack with Jack Leiter, he could not capitalize on a loaded group of performances: 76 from Shea Langeliers, 60 from Big Meat Pete, 83 from Chase Burns, and 64 from Emerson Hancock. Some might call him footpenis.

Having that many players in the top 25 scorers and still losing is absolutely brutal for Moltisanti’s Mistress.

Wagner’s lineup was the deciding factor, putting up an incredible 496-point performance.

The lineup was highlighted by Drake Baldwin with 42 points, Matt Olson with 66, and Aaron Judge with 81, good for the #6 player of the week.

Wagner might begin reaping the benefits of taking Judge #1 overall.

As I highlighted in the quarter-season recap, Judge has been very solid, but he was not putting up those #1 overall pick numbers many expected.

If he can keep this up, Wagner’s team looks increasingly frightening.


🔥 Bryce vs Ice

Bryce Turns It Around, Ice Chokes it Away

Bryce defeats Ice 639.27–562.53, powered by Kris Bubic with 73 points, Ian Happ with 59, and William Contreras with 57.

Bryce was also bitching and moaning early in the week while Ice was up over 120 points & projected around a 75% chance to win.

All Ice had to do was pick up a few pitchers who could net him 25-30 points each & he could've won. We were all set up to start baiting Bryce "Fool's Gold!", "You can't even beat Ice!", "Fraud!".....

But Ice stole that from us. Bryce turned the tide, pulled through, and reminded everyone why he is still sitting up top.

A lot of shit was talked this week between the two gringos, I'm excited for them to matchup again down the road.

🗽 Sarrel Gets His First Win

SARREL DOESN’T LOSE

Sarrel GETS HIS FIRST WIN!!!

Sarrel takes down SlammingMuff 545.37–50.13.

SARREL SLAMMED HIS MUFF!

As I said in the Week 5 recap, SlammingMuff was coming off a brutal loss, and this was Sarrel’s chance to capitalize. He kicked him while he was down and finally gets his first win of the year.

Sarrel was powered by Ozzie Albies, who put up 69 points and finished as the #12 player of the week. He also got 60 points from Kyle Schwarber (aka Dave Kingman - Elite ball knowers will understand the reference), the #19 player of the week.
Dennis Santana killed Slammingmuf this week with a disgraceful -28 points.

Sarrel also made a ballsy pickup, grabbing Elmer Rodriguez, rookie of the Yanks. The move only netted him 10 points on 4 IP, 3 Ks, 4 hits, 4 walks, and 1 hit batsman.

Regardless, Sarrel’s fandom did not cost him this one, as it has in the past.


💪 VinSalv vs Team Lill

VinSalv Finds Pitching

VinSalv takes down Team Lill 680.97–558.9.

VinSalv’s pitching staff was a major issue last week, only netting him 74 points. This week was a completely different story.

Vin’s pitching staff got him 340 points, headlined by a 93-point week from Jesus Luzardo, our #2 scorer of the week.

Additionally, Ben Rice stays hot with a 70-point week. However, an injury is looming after he was removed from Sunday’s game with a hand issue.

Vin’s team will take a big hit if they lose Ben Rice.

One bright takeaway from Team Lill’s squad is Cody Bellinger finally showing a pulse. While he has not been absolutely brutal, he is not even a top-80 player in our league, (although he is paid like a top-20 player).

If Cody can heat up, Lill’s team will become a real threat moving forward.

Johnny Sack hasn’t seen .500 since Week 2… and now gets thrown into another brutal matchup, facing yet ANOTHER top team. Whoever made this schedule is a complete jackass. Let’s see if last year’s champ can finally start a run toward a playoff spot.

Will SlammingMuff bounce back? He needs it BAD after losing to the worst team in the league. SlammingMuff is currently riding a 3-game losing streak, and another loss here could get ugly fast.
