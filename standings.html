Why They Moved

#1 Moltisanti’s Mistress: This is what Power Rankings are for. Three wins in his last four, the hottest offense in the league, and back-to-back statement victories over JohnnySack and Wagner. The record still is not elite, but nobody is playing better baseball right now.

#2 Justin: Two straight losses finally knock Justin off the top spot, but both came while scoring over 700 points. He still leads the league in total scoring by more than 1,000 points and remains one of the league's biggest threats every single week.

#3 Wagner: The standings still say first place, but the Power Rankings tell a different story. Four consecutive weeks under 600 points, a 2–2 stretch over his last four, and a 200-point drubbing by Moltisanti’s Mistress make this the shakiest the league leader has looked all season.

#4 JohnnySack: Huge rise after taking down Justin and moving back over .500. Third in total points, strong recent production, and another statement win added to the resume. JohnnySack is right back in the middle of the playoff hunt.

#5 Bryce: Bryce is still winning, even if it has been ugly. At 9–6, the record deserves respect, but the eye test remains shaky. The standings say contender. The weekly performances say hold your breath.

#6 Team Lill: Drops after falling back under .500. The talent is still there, but the bad timing continues and the rotation has suddenly become a problem. Team Lill is running out of time to turn strong underlying numbers into actual wins.

#7 VinSalv: New season high, 812.93 points, and a massive win over Team Lill. The consistency is still terrifying in the wrong direction, but a week like that has to matter.

#8 Ice: Ice had been heating up, but the narrow loss to Bryce cools things down a bit. The roster looks better than it did earlier in the year, but the playoff hill is still steep.

#9 SlammingMuff: Got back in the win column, but it came against Sarrel. Still ASS.

#10 Sarrel: Still last. Still ASS.


📐 How It’s Calculated

Power rankings are not just based on record. They reflect overall team strength, performance trends, and a little bit of commissioner judgment.

  • 35% — Record
  • 30% — Total Points
  • 20% — Last 2 Weeks
  • 10% — Highest Weekly Score
  • 5% — Eye Test

If you have a problem with the rankings, win more games, score more points, or as Uncle Paulie would say “Shut the fuck up about it!”.
