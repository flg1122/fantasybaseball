Why They Moved

#1 Wagner: Still the best record, most points, and highest weekly score. The trade was moronic, but the resume still holds for now.

#2 Team Lill: Big jump after an 827-point week. The rotation looks nasty, the team looks balanced, Team Lill is trending up hard.

#3 Bryce: Still 5–2 and top three in standings, but Week 7 was uninspiring. Framber nuked him, the wire was ugly, and the team did not look scary.

#4 Moltisanti’s Mistress: Injuries knock him down on the eye test, and losing to Sarrel hurts. Still, the points and last-two-week production are elite.

#5 Justin: 6–1 record, but the schedule merchant allegations are impossible to ignore. Winning with less than 560 points is not moving anyone.

#6 Sarrel: Biggest riser. Back-to-back wins, a 748-point week, and suddenly this team is not a complete joke anymore.

#7 SlammingMuff: Gets credit for beating Bryce, but 535 points is nothing to write home about.

#8 Johnny Sack: Robbed again. Scored 725 and would have beaten more than half the league, but 1–6 is still disgustingly embarassing.

#9 VinSalv: Looked legit last week. This week, not so much. Roster management remains a crime scene.

#10 Ice: Bottom three in points and does not scare anyone. Dylan Cease can only do so much.



📐 How It’s Calculated

Power rankings are not just based on record. They reflect overall team strength, performance trends, and a little bit of commissioner judgment.

  • 35% — Record
  • 30% — Total Points
  • 20% — Last 2 Weeks
  • 10% — Highest Weekly Score
  • 5% — Eye Test

If you have a problem with the rankings, win more games, score more points, or as Uncle Paulie would say “Shut the fuck up about it!”.
