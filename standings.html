Why They Moved

#1 Justin: The schedule merchant allegations are not dead, but 7–1 with a 732-point week is hard to ignore. Justin takes the top spot after Wagner looked shaky and Justin reminded the league he can still put up a real score.

#2 Wagner: Still 7–1, still first in points, still holding the best weekly score of the season. But the team looks weaker after the trades, Max Fried is on the IL, and barely beating Sarrel does not scream dominant.

#3 Moltisanti’s Mistress: Huge bounce-back win over Bryce. The injury concerns are real, but Moltisanti’s Mistress still has elite scoring ability and remains one of the most dangerous teams in the league.

#4 SlammingMuff: Biggest riser near the top. Back-to-back wins over Bryce and Team Lill changed the conversation fast. SlammingMuff is suddenly looking like a real playoff threat.

#5 Team Lill: The rotation still looks scary, but the lineup concerns are getting louder. Losing directly to SlammingMuff knocks Team Lill out of the top tier for now.

#6 JohnnySack: Finally got back in the win column and posted the highest score of Week 8. The record is still ugly, but JohnnySack is now third in total points scored. Nobody wants to play this team right now.

#7 Bryce: Full-on free fall. Two straight ugly scoring weeks, two straight losses, and managers are starting to realize this roster might just be Shohei Ohtani and vibes.

#8 Sarrel: Still competitive, but the wins are not coming. Sarrel had started to look much better lately, but that was wiped away this week, moving him back down.

#9 Ice: Ice put up a respectable score, but the team keeps losing and the waiver decisions remain terrifying. Three straight losses says enough.

#10 VinSalv: 282 points this week. There is no spin zone for that. Lineup negligence is real. Scoring 735 points over the last 2 weeks (which most teams can score in 1 week) is UGLY.

Power rankings are not just based on record. They reflect overall team strength, performance trends, and a little bit of commissioner judgment.

  • 35% — Record
  • 30% — Total Points
  • 20% — Last 2 Weeks
  • 10% — Highest Weekly Score
