Why They Moved

#1 JohnnySack: The climb from last place to the top of the Power Rankings is complete. JohnnySack has won eight of his last ten, owns the second-highest point total in the league, and just ran through the ultimate gauntlet by beating Justin and Wagner in back-to-back weeks. He did not simply survive the league’s top two teams—he steamrolled them. Nobody is playing better fantasy baseball right now.

#2 Bryce: The fraud investigation has officially been closed. Bryce has won three straight, just dropped a new season-high 921.67 points, and now sits comfortably at 10–6. The record is strong, the production is back, and this team finally looks like a legitimate contender again.

#3 Justin: Justin returned to first place, still leads the league in total scoring by more than 1,000 points, and owns the highest single-week score of the season. However, a pair of losses followed by an uninspiring escape against VinSalv prevents him from reclaiming the top spot in the Power Rankings.

#4 Moltisanti’s Mistress: One terrible week does not erase the month of offensive dominance that came before it. Moltisanti’s Mistress falls after being upset by Sarrel, but the recent tear and overall scoring production keep him safely inside the top four. We are not worried after one bad week.

#5 Wagner: The record still says 12–4, but everything else is setting off alarms. Five straight weeks without cracking 620 points, a 2–3 record during that stretch, back-to-back losses, and a beatdown from JohnnySack have Wagner officially on fraud alert. The standings say contender. The recent box scores say suspect.

#6 Team Lill: Team Lill gets back to .500 after surviving Ice in one of the best matchups of the week. The production remains competitive, the ceiling is still high, and this team remains firmly in the playoff race. The problem is that almost every victory seems to require surviving an absolute war.

#7 Ice: Ice moves up despite losing again. He posted a new season-high 738.67 points, suffered his second straight painfully close loss, and earned the Most Points in a Loss award. The record is ugly, but the team has clearly turned a corner. Unfortunately, the fantasy gods continue kicking him in the teeth.

#8 VinSalv: VinSalv followed his 812.93-point explosion with a respectable 561.07 against Justin, but the inconsistency remains impossible to trust. The ceiling is real. The floor is terrifying. Nobody knows which version is showing up from week to week.

#9 SlammingMuff: Still ASS.

#10 Sarrel: Still ASS.


📐 How It’s Calculated

Power rankings are not just based on record. They reflect overall team strength, performance trends, and a little bit of commissioner judgment.

  • 35% — Record
  • 30% — Total Points
  • 20% — Last 2 Weeks
  • 10% — Highest Weekly Score
  • 5% — Eye Test

If you have a problem with the rankings, win more games, score more points, or as Uncle Paulie would say “Shut the fuck up about it!”.
