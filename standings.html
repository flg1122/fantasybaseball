Why They Moved

#1 Justin: The schedule merchant allegations have officially been silenced for now. Justin dropped the first 1000-point week of the season and now owns the highest score of the year. This team looks terrifying again.

#2 Wagner: Eight straight wins is impossible to ignore, but Wagner still does not look dominant. The rotation continues carrying a lineup that frankly looks rough, and the injury issues are piling up.

#3 Team Lill: Big rise after taking down Moltisanti’s Mistress and climbing into third place. The lineup finally backed up the elite rotation and this team suddenly looks dangerous again.

#4 Moltisanti’s Mistress: Slides slightly after losing to Team Lill, but this roster still scores too many points to fall far. One of the better overall teams in the league with third most points, despite the record.

#5 JohnnySack: Quietly becoming one of the hottest teams in the league. Back-to-back wins, fourth in total points scored, and the lineup finally looks alive. Nobody wants this matchup right now.

#6 Bryce: The losing streak hit three games, but Bryce still holds a better record and more total points than most of the middle-tier teams. The problem is the roster still feels way too dependent on Shohei Ohtani carrying everything.

#7 SlammingMuff: Week 8 momentum came crashing back down to earth after getting obliterated by Justin. Still competitive, but the resume no longer justifies sitting above Bryce or JohnnySack.

#8 Sarrel: Competitive moments are there, but the losses continue stacking up. The pitching still lacks depth and the playoff picture is starting to get ugly.

#9 VinSalv: Congratulations on winning the Mid-Off. VinSalv finally got back in the win column and at least showed signs of life, which is more than Ice can currently say.

#10 Ice: Four straight losses, injuries everywhere, and the roster looks completely broken. Losing Vladimir Guerrero Jr. and Dylan Cease only made an already ugly situation worse.


📐 How It’s Calculated

Power rankings are not just based on record. They reflect overall team strength, performance trends, and a little bit of commissioner judgment.

  • 35% — Record
  • 30% — Total Points
  • 20% — Last 2 Weeks
  • 10% — Highest Weekly Score
  • 5% — Eye Test
