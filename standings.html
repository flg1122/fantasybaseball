Why They Moved

#1 Justin: Back-to-back nuclear weeks and now sitting at 9–1. Justin looks like the best team in the league entering the biggest matchup of the season.

#2 Wagner: Nine straight wins keeps Wagner locked at #2. The lineup still has questions, but the rotation keeps dragging this team across the finish line.

#3 Team Lill: Took a tough loss to Wagner, but still put up 670 and remains one of the stronger teams by total points and recent production.

#4 Moltisanti’s Mistress: The record is ugly, but the points are still elite. Losing with 711 hurts, but this roster still has too much firepower to fall far.

#5 JohnnySack: Three straight wins and climbing back into the race. The record is still below .500, but the production and momentum are real.

#6 Bryce: Finally stopped the bleeding with a 735-point week. The roster is still beat up, so he only gets a modest bump, but a win is a win.

#7 VinSalv: Biggest riser of the week after dropping 749 and moving back to .500. Still inconsistent, but this was a statement week.

#8 SlammingMuff: Drops after getting absolutely destroyed by VinSalv. Back-to-back down weeks have the Muff sliding at the wrong time.

#9 Ice: Moves up only because Sarrel looks worse. Ice’s lineup finally woke up, but the pitching staff completely betrayed him.

#10 Sarrel: Three straight losses, no waiver activity, and barely 794 points over the last two weeks. Sarrel looks asleep at the wheel again.


📐 How It’s Calculated

Power rankings are not just based on record. They reflect overall team strength, performance trends, and a little bit of commissioner judgment.

  • 35% — Record
  • 30% — Total Points
  • 20% — Last 2 Weeks
  • 10% — Highest Weekly Score
  • 5% — Eye Test
