Why They Moved

#1 Justin: Still the best team in the league by the formula. He leads the league in points by a mile, owns the highest weekly score, and even after a loss, still looks terrifying.

#2 Wagner: Best record in the league at 12–2, but three straight weeks under 600 points keeps him below Justin. The wins are real, but the recent production is shaky.

#3 Moltisanti’s Mistress: Huge jump after a monstrous Week 14. Second in total points, back to .500, and finally looking like the dangerous roster we expected all season.

#4 Bryce: Bryce gets credit for stabilizing and moving to 8–6. The team had been slipping, but a 768-point week and strong overall record push him back up.

#5 Team Lill: Drops (from where he was atfer week 10) despite a massive win over Justin. The recent production is excellent, but the record and total points keep him just behind Bryce and Moltisanti’s Mistress.

#6 JohnnySack: The six-game heater finally ended. Still one of the better scoring teams in the league, but the loss knocks him down in a crowded middle tier.

#7 Ice: Biggest riser near the bottom. Three straight wins, 722 this week, and suddenly Ice looks alive after spending most of the season buried.

#8 VinSalv: 0 upside, inconsistency is brutal. One week he looks dangerous, the next week he looks like he forgot fantasy baseball exists.

#9 SlammingMuff: Drops after another ugly week and another missed opportunity. Shit.

#10 Sarrel: Still last. Shit.


📐 How It’s Calculated

Power rankings are not just based on record. They reflect overall team strength, performance trends, and a little bit of commissioner judgment.

  • 35% — Record
  • 30% — Total Points
  • 20% — Last 2 Weeks
  • 10% — Highest Weekly Score
  • 5% — Eye Test
