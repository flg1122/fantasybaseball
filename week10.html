Week 10 Recap

Justin Keeps Rolling

Week 10 gave us another Justin explosion, another Wagner escape act, JohnnySack keeping hope alive, Bryce finally stopping the bleeding, and VinSalv absolutely slamming Jon's Muff.


🔥 Week 10 Snapshot

The Chase Pack Tightens

Justin and Wagner continue to look like the class of the league, improving to 9–1 and extending their massive winning streaks. But behind them, the standings have become an absolute dogfight.

Bryce finally stopped the bleeding, Team Lill dropped back to .500 after another tough matchup, VinSalv suddenly finds himself back in the playoff conversation, and JohnnySack's three-game winning streak has turned what once looked like a lost season into a legitimate push. Meanwhile, Moltisanti’s Mistress and SlammingMuff continue to slide, Ice can't buy a win, and Sarrel looks completely asleep at the wheel.

With only one game separating several teams in the middle of the standings, the chase pack is tightening fast.

🏆 Matchup of the Week Recap

Wagner Survives Team Lill

Wagner defeats Team Lill 720.63 – 670.3

Starting with the matchup I highlighted coming into the week: Wagner versus Team Lill. This was a strong matchup on paper, and it delivered.

At this point, Team Lill may be starting to take over JohnnySack's title as the league's patron saint of bad beats. Team Lill has been involved in marquee matchups for weeks now, and even when he posts a strong score, he keeps running into teams that do just enough to send him home with another frustrating loss.

Team Lill outscored Wagner’s lineup 340 to 286, but could not pull through because Wagner’s pitching carried him once again.

Wagner had the #1 scorer of the week in Jacob Misiorowski, who dropped 124.6 points. He also got 54.9 from Justin Wrobleski and 58.7 from O’Neill Cruz.

On the Team Lill side, he had Jonathan Aranda, the #7 player of the week, with 89.7 points. Team Lill had enough firepower to make this interesting, but Wagner’s rotation was the difference.

Right now, it really sucks to be Team Lill (aka The Schedules Favorite Victim). Good team, tough schedule, brutal timing.

🚨 Statement Win

Justin Demolishes Moltisanti’s Mistress

Justin defeats Moltisanti’s Mistress 942.97 – 711.3

Justin once again demolished another top-tier team in the league. Back-to-back weeks near 1000 points is ridiculous, and despite all the schedule merchant claims, it may be time to admit Justin might just be the best team in the league.

Moltisanti’s Mistress was looking to get right and make a statement after falling to Team Lill last week. His best efforts were not enough.

Jarren Duran gave him 77.2 points on 11 hits and four homers. Yordan Alvarez added 86.7 on eight hits and five homers. Somehow, that still was not enough.

Moltisanti’s Mistress outscored Justin’s lineup 470.52 to 343.11. You would think that would be enough to win. Then Justin’s rotation said hold my beer and put up 599.87 points compared to Moltisanti’s 240.8.

This brings us right back to the biggest lesson of this league: pitching, pitching, pitching.

Justin got 58 from a healthy-looking Gerrit Cole, 40 from trade acquisition Jhoan Duran, and somehow 41 from Jack Flaherty. The list goes on. Tough week to run into Justin. You look at his stats and they barely seem real.

Putting up 711 points and losing by over 230 should not be possible. Yet somehow, Justin made it look routine.

📈 Win Streak Watch

JohnnySack Keeps It Alive

JohnnySack defeats Ice 653.5 – 609.8

JohnnySack keeps the win streak alive, defeating Ice in the closest matchup of the week. Somehow, Ice’s lineup finally woke up, put up 430 offensive points, and he still lost. That is hilarious.

Ice was absolutely boned by his pitching staff: Bailey Ober -30.57, Zac Gallen -19.7, and Zack Wheeler -12.8. If those guys do not blow up, Ice wins this matchup.

Ice’s lineup finally exploded this week. One of the worst offenses in the league decided to come alive against JohnnySack, of course. Will Smith put up 50, Jeremy Peña put up 66, and Fernando Tatis Jr. decided it was finally time to hit his first home run of the year, putting up 51.

Ice’s offense finished with 430 points compared to JohnnySack’s 247. But JohnnySack’s rotation demolished this week with a monstrous 406 points, led by Keider Montero with 64.03, Joe Ryan with 43.43, and Tatsuya Imai with 64.7.

JohnnySack moves to 4–6, while Ice drops to 2–8. The luck seems to be turning for JohnnySack, finally getting a favorable matchup. It might be time to call it wraps for Team Ice.

🛑 Bleeding Stopped

Bryce Gets Right Against Sarrel

Bryce defeats Sarrel 735.37 – 403.83

Bryce finally gets right, stops the bleeding of a three-week losing streak, and takes down Sarrel.

Bryce was powered by 82 from James Wood, 81 from Shohei Ohtani, and 91 from Sean Burke. For now, Bryce puts the fraud questions to rest.

Sarrel, on the other hand, looks like he has reverted to his old self. He once again left all four waiver claims untouched and appeared completely asleep at the wheel.

At this point, Sarrel’s wins feel less like active management and more like blind luck. He has talent, but the lack of moves is killing him. Just sad all around for Sarrel.

💥 Muff Slammed

VinSalv Destroys SlammingMuff

VinSalv defeats SlammingMuff 749.7 – 427.93

VinSalv absolutely slammed Jon's Muff.

VinSalv’s lineup exploded, putting up 419.3 points compared to SlammingMuff’s 151. His rotation also handled business, putting up 330.4 to SlammingMuff's 276.23.

VinSalv was powered by Ronald Acuña Jr. with 95.9, and Bryan Reynolds with 90, and Ben Rice with 85.6. 3 players in this week's Top 10, pretty outstanding.

Shota Imanaga continues to be a problem for SlammingMuff, giving him -15 points this week. Braxton Ashcraft was outstanding with 70.77, and Yoshinobu Yamamoto continued to be effective with 40.07.

Unfortunately, that was not nearly enough for SlammingMuff to overcome the power of Salv.

🔮 Week 11 Preview:

🔥 Matchup of the Week

Justin @ Wagner

It does not get bigger than this.

Two teams sit at 9–1. Two teams are riding massive win streaks. Two teams have spent most of the season battling for the top spot in the standings. And now they finally collide.

Justin looks like an absolute powerhouse right now, coming off back-to-back monster performances and looking every bit like the league's best team. Wagner, meanwhile, continues to survive despite injuries, questionable trades, and a roster that somehow refuses to die.

The winner takes sole possession of first place and becomes the first team in the league to reach 10 wins.

These are also the league's two highest-scoring teams. No excuses. No schedule merchant allegations. No luck arguments. Just two heavyweights stepping into the ring.

There is also a little history here.

Wagner handed Justin his only loss of the season back in Week 2. Since that defeat, Justin has not lost a single matchup and has ripped off an incredible 8-game winning streak.

Now Justin gets his chance at revenge. Was Week 2 the start of Wagner's dominance, or was it the spark that turned Justin into the league's most dangerous team?

One of these win streaks ends this week.

Bryce @ JohnnySack

JohnnySack enters Week 11 riding a 3-game winning streak and suddenly finds himself back in the playoff conversation.

Bryce finally got back into the win column last week, but it came against a struggling Sarrel squad. Now he faces a much tougher challenge against one of the hottest teams in the league.

JohnnySack would love to keep the streak rolling and gets a shot at a Bryce team that has looked shaky over the past few weeks while battling injuries throughout the roster.

Bryce still sits in third place at 6–4, but that cushion is shrinking fast. With multiple teams sitting at 5–5 and looking to make a move, another loss could make things very uncomfortable.

Perhaps the most shocking stat of all: JohnnySack has actually scored more points this season than Bryce despite sitting two games behind him in the standings.

Moltisanti's Mistress @ VinSalv

Moltisanti's Mistress enters this matchup riding a brutal 2-game losing streak.

Consecutive matchups against Team Lill and Justin have not done him any favors, and this feels like a prime opportunity to bounce back, reassert himself, and get back into the win column.

VinSalv, however, has quietly won two straight after taking down Ice and then absolutely dismantling SlammingMuff.

What looked like a dead season a few weeks ago suddenly has life. VinSalv has climbed back to .500 and finds himself right in the middle of the playoff race.

This one is sneaky good and could have major implications for the middle of the standings.

SlammingMuff @ Ice

Well, well, well.

The old golf course grudge match returns.

The all-time series between these two managers is tied 2–2, and SlammingMuff already took down Ice earlier this season.

SlammingMuff enters on a two-game losing streak and desperately needs a bounce-back performance. Meanwhile, Ice has now dropped five straight and is running out of time to save his season.

It always seems like struggling teams circle Ice on the schedule and think, "Yep, that's exactly what I need right now."

Of course, Ice probably feels the same way. If there was ever a team to get right against, it might be SlammingMuff as summer has just begun.

Because as league history has taught us, once the sun comes out, the boat hits the water, and the Silverado starts flying down the Parkway, SlammingMuff suddenly becomes vulnerable and occasionally forgets fantasy baseball exists.

Rivalry. Losing streaks. Desperation. Golf. What more could you ask for?

Team Lill @ Sarrel

Team Lill can finally take a deep breath.

After spending weeks battling contenders and suffering through one brutal matchup after another, Team Lill finds what should be a softer landing spot against Sarrel.

Sarrel is currently riding a 3-game losing streak and continues to look asleep at the wheel, but every losing streak eventually ends.

Team Lill needs a bounce-back win after another tough loss to Wagner. Sarrel needs proof that his season still has a pulse.

Will Team Lill finally catch a break, or will Sarrel wake up just in time to burst Team Lill's bubble?

Final Take:

Week 11 features the biggest matchup of the season so far as Justin and Wagner battle for sole possession of first place.

Meanwhile, Bryce and JohnnySack could shake up the playoff picture, Moltisanti's Mistress desperately needs a win, and the SlammingMuff-Ice rivalry gets another chapter.

The standings are starting to shake up, and every matchup now feels a little more important.
