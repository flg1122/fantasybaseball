Week 7 Recap

Top Teams Get Toppled

Week 7 delivered chaos across the league. Heavyweights fell, underdogs fought back, managers burned waiver claims like lottery tickets, and one desperate blockbuster trade may have changed the entire season.


🔥 League Chaos

The Tides Turn

Week 7 felt like the moment this season truly came alive. Bryce finally bled. Justin survived another matchup he probably should not have won. Sarrel suddenly looks competent. Wagner nearly collapsed under pressure before unloading a desperation trade package that shook the entire league at 1 AM Sunday morning.

🏆 Matchup of the Week

Sarrel Stuns Moltisanti’s Mistress

Sarrel defeats Moltisanti’s Mistress 749.93 - 709.03

Sarrel stays hot against Moltisanti’s Mistress and remains in the win column. Two weeks, two wins for Sarrel.

This matchup stayed tight deep into Sunday, with Sarrel up 716–684 at one point while Moltisanti still had Eduardo Rodriguez going. But Sarrel had the firepower to hold on.

Bobby Witt Jr. put up 78.4 points, finishing as the #8 overall player and #2 hitter of the week. Bryce Harper went nuclear with 81.9 points, finishing as the #4 overall player and #1 hitter of the week. Kyle Schwarber also stayed hot, finishing as the #4 hitter and #15 overall player with 66.7 points. George Kirby added 45, and Janson Junk chipped in 46, helping lead Sarrel to his second win of the year.

Harper was removed from Saturday’s game against the Rockies due to a migraine. Seems like vaginitis to me.

Sarrel only used one waiver claim this week, truly solidifying himself as a careless, weak, arrogant manager. And somehow, it worked.

On the Moltisanti side, costly injuries have started to derail what looked like one of the most dangerous teams in the league. Tarik Skubal needs surgery, and who knows when he will be back. Ryan Helsley hit the 15-day IL with an elbow issue, and Steven Matz, who Moltisanti already dropped, also has an elbow injury. Despite having the best pitcher of the week, Cristopher Sanchez, who finished as the #1 overall player and #1 pitcher with 115.6 points, & the #3 pitcher of the week, Eduardo Rodriguez with 101.87 points Moltisanti could not get it done.

Somehow, Moltisanti’s Mistress had TWO of the top three overall players of the entire week — Cristopher Sanchez and Eduardo Rodriguez — and still walked away with a loss. That is the kind of defeat that turns a season.

Moltisanti’s Week 8 waiver claims may determine whether this team remains a top threat.

Two weeks ago, Sarrel looked dead in the water. Now? Managers might actually have to start taking him seriously.

🚨 Trade Alert

1 AM Blockbuster: Wagner Panics

On Sunday around 1 AM, Wagner completely lost his mind. Terrified of blowing his matchup against JohnnySack, Wagner panic-fired off one of the wildest overpays this league has ever seen — all to gain one extra starter for Sunday afternoon. The funniest part? He ended up not even needing him.

The deal:

Justin sends

Emmet Sheehan, Justin Wrobleski, Austin Riley

Wagner sends

Jhoan Duran, Kyle Tucker

What Wagner was thinking here? I’m not so sure.

Austin Riley has been abysmal. Maybe Wagner was trying to buy low? Usually you do not buy low by spending A METRIC TON.

Wagner essentially looked at his roster, saw Kyle Tucker and Jhoan Duran, and decided, “You know what? I would rather have two guys that were literally sitting on waivers two weeks ago.” A truly inspirational level of panic.

Wagner sent a top outfielder, literally one of the highest-paid players in the MLB, and the #2 closer in ALL OF BASEBALL. In return, he got back a declining Austin Riley (.650 OPS), Emmet Sheehan (4.79 ERA), and early-season overachiever Justin Wrobleski.

This trade felt less like roster management and more like Justin sticking Wagner up at gunpoint.

As commissioner, I genuinely considered sending this trade to the league office for psychological evaluation. But at the end of the day, if Wagner wants to dismantle the team he built around Judge and donate elite talent directly into Justin’s lap, that is ultimately his right as an American.

Everybody - a moment of silence, mourning the death of Wagner's team.

💪 Team Lill vs Ice

Team Lill Decimates Ice

Team Lill defeats Ice 827.4 - 488.03

No sugarcoating this, Team Lill beat the shit out of Ice, reaching a 100% win probability by Saturday evening (it could've been even sooner I honestly wasn't watching). This was the kind of matchup all managers dream of.

For Team Lill, Cody Bellinger (60.7 pts) is staying hot! Just like I said last week, IF this guy gets & stays hot, watch out for this team. Murakami has cooled off a bit, but he is still producing, with 43 points on the week. Josh Naylor was on fire with 64 points.

Cal Raleigh, however, was an absolute dumpster fire this week. He finished with an 0-for-20 bid and -17 points. Brutal.

Cam Schlittler continues to shine in Team Lill’s rotation alongside Paul Skenes — and at this point, Lill may legitimately have the best rotation in the league. Schlittler has been dominant, racking up 73 points to claim the #9 overall player spot and the #7 ranked pitcher. Skenes somehow turned just ONE start into 68 points, good for the #11 pitcher ranking. And then there’s Michael Wacha, quietly leading the charge with 81 points — ranking #5 overall and #4 among all pitchers. That trio is becoming a serious problem for the rest of the league.

The -40 from Tony Santillan could have been detrimental, but it did not matter.

For Ice, there were no true blowups, but also no big bangs. Just average production straight down the board. “Five Inning Freddy” Peralta did not prove anyone wrong with another five-inning outing, totaling a measly 22 points. Not exactly what you expect from your sixth-round pick.

I will say Dylan Cease has been tremendous for Ice. Staying consistent and mounting 58 points for Ice. But it just was not enough.

💥 Upset Watch! Fraud Alert?

SlammingMuff Takes Down Bryce

SlammingMuff defeats Bryce 535.07 - 468.97

SlammingMuff takes down a top team in Bryce, and this was exactly the kind of win Jon needed.

He was powered by an impressive performance from Nathan Eovaldi 52 points. Jon did not forget to play him this week. Last week, Jon gave the Yankees way too much credit and benched Eovaldi when he pitched against them, for some mind-numbing reason.

Thirty-six-year-old Freddie Freeman proved he still has something left in the tank, putting up 31+ points.

Former Met Jacob deGrom put the cherry on top for Jon, putting together a beautiful performance and finishing the week with 79+ points. He finished as the #7 overall player and #6 pitcher.

For Bryce, Framber Valdez was absolutely abysmal. He blew up and proved not only is he trash, but he is also a scumbag by drilling Trevor Story because his feelings were hurt. -41 points. Bum.

The bloom has fallen off Jose Soriano’s rose, as we all expected. An average pitcher who was wildly overperforming got brought back down to earth with -14 points to start Bryce's week Monday on 4 IP, 5 Ks, 8 hits, 3 walks, and 2 ding dongs.

He mildly recovered with a 50+ point outing Sunday, but it was not enough to carry Bryce.

Bryce burned all four waiver claims this week — Soroka -22, Ray -23, Castillo -1, and Buehler +28 — It was a rough week on the wire to say the least. A few blowups and an underwhelming performance by Logan Webb (4 pts) led to his demise.

Logan Webb also heads to the 15-day IL. A true innings eater in today’s game, and that could prove to be a costly loss in the coming weeks for Bryce.

🍰 Schedule Merchant Watch

Justin Survives VinSalv

Justin defeats VinSalv 556.73 - 452.57

In the beginning of the week, it looked like VinSalv was all set up to beat Justin. Midweek, Vin had over a 60% win probability.

And then he absolutely blew it.

VinSalv’s managing to lose, was almost as surprising as Wagner accepting that trade. Justin had brutal roster inefficiency, highlighted by massive negative performances from Andrew Painter (-32.97), Noah Schultz (-9), and Will Warren (-8). Despite that level of roster drag, Vin still couldn’t close the door.

On top of that, VinSalv had elite production sitting in his lineup — including Andy Pages, the #8 overall player and #2 hitter, who dropped 74 points. Even with that kind of firepower, he still couldn’t pull through.

Vin’s catcher Agustin Ramirez, was sent to the minors on May 4th and is still on his roster as of Sunday, May 10th. Vin’s roster management is absolutely mind-boggling.

I think a monkey could do better.

Justin was powered by Seiya Suzuki with 46 points. Who Justin called "a washed bum" in another early-week mental breakdown. Such a bitch.

Nick Kurtz also continues his bid to prove he is a generational talent, putting up 48+ points.

In the end, Justin did not win this one — VinSalv lost it himself. Especially given Justin’s roster actively worked against him, yet the ultra-weak schedule once again carried the result.

⚔️ Wagner vs JohnnySack

JohnnySack Falls Short Again

Wagner defeats JohnnySack 864.9 - 722.53

Friday night, JohnnySack trailed Wagner by more than 100 points, holding just a 22% win probability. By Saturday morning, that number had climbed to 40% after a huge Friday performance.

JohnnySack exploded for 198 points on Friday behind strong outings from Michael McGreevy with 53, Nolan McLean with 30, and Keider Montero with 41.

He kept the momentum rolling Saturday, adding another 180 points, fueled by more quality pitching performances from Randy Vasquez with 33, Joe Ryan with 37, and Clay Holmes with 34.

By Saturday evening, JohnnySack had completely flipped the matchup — taking a 620–547 lead and boosting his win probability to 82%.

Then came Sunday.

Wagner had Cade Cavalli, Bubba Chandler, Bryce Elder, Logan Gilbert, Davis Martin, Justin Wrobleski, and Jameson Taillon going. Seven starting pitchers against JohnnySack’s three: Payton Tolle, Carlos Rodon, and Sandy Alcantara.

Up 73 points, all JohnnySack could do was hope and pray some of Wagner’s pitchers collapsed.

Of course, that did not happen.

Tolle sucked & got 14, Rodon stunk it up and got 16, and Alcantara did well with 31. But Wagner’s pitching is what took him over the top.

Logan Gilbert dropped 59, as Chicago looked lost and disabled. Taillon got 28, despite being objectively a bad pitcher. Cade Cavalli who actually sucks balls got 27. Bryce Elder added 34, even though the Dodgers usually rake him. Not today, of course, because that is what JohnnySack needed.

Mystery man Davis Martin dropped 45, because Seattle blows and made him look like Cy Young. Wrobleski added 29, getting brought back down to earth a bit in this start, but that was a non-factor.

Even more brutal? Despite Sunday’s rough finish, Payton Tolle still ended the week as the #11 overall player. JohnnySack got elite production across the board and still could not survive Wagner’s pitching avalanche.

JohnnySack had the comeback lined up. But Wagner had the arms. Once again, JohnnySack faced the top scorer of the week and was left staring at a matchup that slipped away — despite posting a score that would’ve beaten 60% of the league.

🔮 Week 8 Preview:

Matchup of the Week:

Moltisanti’s Mistress @ Bryce

Both teams are coming off ugly losses. Both teams desperately need this win. Maybe Moltisanti’s Mistress even more than Bryce. With Moltisanti dealing with major pitching injuries, his waiver wire moves this week may determine whether he can remain one of the league’s top threats. This one is the Matchup of the Week in my opinion.

Wagner @ Sarrel

Once again, same as last week, Sarrel picks up a win and immediately gets thrown into the ringer — this time against Wagner. He proved he could handle Moltisanti’s Mistress. Now the question becomes: can he handle Wagner? Two wins is a streak. Three wins would be a sign things are turning for Sarrel.

JohnnySack @ VinSalv

JohnnySack is riding a brutal 6-game losing streak and sitting in last place. With 4,702.467 points scored against him this year he has truly had the worst luck in the league. If he does not get this win, his season may genuinely be over. Luckily for him, with the way VinSalv manages his lineup, JohnnySack should be in good shape to finally get back in the win column. This is desperation versus roster malpractice.

Justin @ Ice

Another cakewalk. Another walk in the park matchup for Justin. How lucky can somebody be? Ice is currently riding a 2-game losing streak and is bottom 3 in total ponts. Ice desperately needs to bounce back. Unfortunately for the rest of the league, Justin once again appears to have found another soft landing spot. The schedule merchant allegations are not slowing down anytime soon.

Team Lill @ SlammingMuff

Two teams coming off big wins. In Week 6, they both took brutal losses. In Week 7, they both bounced back. SlammingMuff would LOVE to move to 4–4 and drag Team Lill right back down to 4-4 with him. Winner starts looking like a legitimate playoff threat. Loser gets dragged back into the middle-of-the-pack chaos.

Final Take:

Week 7 completely shifted the feel of the league. Top teams finally got punched in the mouth, the standings tightened up, and suddenly teams that looked dead a few weeks ago are starting to show signs of life.

We are officially entering the point of the season where every matchup starts to matter a little more. Some teams are heating up. Others are spiraling. And a few managers are one bad week away from completely losing it.
