🏆 Matchup of the Week Recap

JohnnySack Knocks Wagner Out of First

JohnnySack defeats Wagner 820.63 – 616

It is officially worry time for Wagner.

This is now five straight weeks without cracking 620 points. Wagner is just 2–3 over that stretch, and while the record itself is not disastrous, the collapse in production from a team that once looked completely dominant is impossible to ignore.

🚨 FRAUD ALERT. The sirens are officially on. Five straight weeks of mediocre production, the offense has vanished, and the aura that carried Wagner through the first half of the season is fading fast. Earlier this year, teams circled Wagner on the schedule hoping to survive. Now they're circling him thinking, "This is winnable." The record still says elite, but lately the box scores have been screaming suspect.

JohnnySack was powered by another tremendous pitching performance, with his rotation crushing Wagner’s 576.13–327.4.

Outstanding starts from Shane Baz with 60.13, Shane Drohan with 57.37, Payton Tolle with 78.53, and Kevin Gausman with 68.47 completely sealed the deal.

Wagner did not suffer any true disasters from his rotation, but the production was painfully average. Nothing exploded, nobody carried the team, and against a pitching staff like JohnnySack’s, mid-tier production was never going to be enough.

The lineup numbers may be even more concerning. Wagner only outscored JohnnySack’s hitters 288.6–244.5, despite JohnnySack playing the week down three outfielders.

Wagner’s lineup once looked like the strength of the roster. Now the offense has become another problem that may need to be addressed before this skid turns into a full-blown collapse.

On the other side, JohnnySack has to be thrilled. What once looked like the most cursed team in the league has climbed from last place all the way into fourth, now sitting at 9–7 with the second-most points scored.

Wagner falls to 12–4 and, for the first time in what feels like forever, has officially been knocked out of first place.

💥 Fraud Watch Cancelled

Bryce Absolutely Destroys SlammingMuff

Bryce defeats SlammingMuff 921.67 – 412.33

The fraud watch has officially shifted from Bryce to Wagner. Bryce can finally breathe after absolutely dismantling SlammingMuff in one of the biggest beatdowns of the season.

Bryce dominated this matchup from top to bottom. His lineup outscored Jon's 437.2–310.9, while his pitching staff completely buried SlammingMuff's rotation 484.47–101.43.

Jon left 67 points sitting on his bench, but let's be honest—it would not have mattered. Bryce was raining a shit storm down on him this week, and nothing was going to stop it.

Bryce was led by the #1 player of the week, James Wood, who exploded for 116.7 points. Wood continues to put together an unbelievable season and now sits at 815.3 total points, good for the #2 outfielder in the league.

The onslaught continued with 65 from Jake Bauers, 61 from Ryan O'Hearn, 68 from Shohei Ohtani, and 65 from Hunter Greene. There simply was no weak spot in Bryce's lineup.

As for Jon... what more is there to say? His season has been over for weeks. No amount of waiver claims, lineup setting, trade proposals, motivational speeches, or boat rides down the Jersey Shore are fixing this disaster.

Bryce, meanwhile, improves to 10–6, extends his winning streak to three games, and maintains his grip on third place, one game ahead of the surging JohnnySack.

Fraud alert? Case dismissed. Bryce is back!

😮 Survive and Advance

Justin Escapes VinSalv

Justin defeats VinSalv 593.9 – 561.07

The top two teams in the league (Justin & Wagner) continue to look... human.

After dropping two straight, this felt like the perfect opportunity for Justin to remind everyone why he has led the league in points all season. Instead, he limped to 593.9 points against VinSalv of all teams. A win is a win, but this was far from the statement performance many expected.

Justin also suffered a brutal blow this week, losing Nick Kurtz, currently the #2 first baseman in the league. Losing that kind of production this late in the season could have major playoff implications.

Truthfully, there was not much to write home about in this matchup. Outside of Ben Rice absolutely going nuclear with 100.3 points, good for the #3 player of the week, the rest of the box score was pretty underwhelming.

VinSalv got just 19 points from Jazz Chisholm Jr., while Justin managed only 17 from Christian Walker. Will Warren posted -16 for Justin, while Kumar Rocker finished at -12 for VinSalv. It was ugly baseball all around.

This game genuinely could have gone either way. Instead, Justin catches another break, survives by just over thirty points, and vaults right back into first place thanks to Wagner's loss.

Two straight losses followed by a less-than-convincing escape against a struggling VinSalv squad... the storm clouds may finally be rolling in over Team Justin. Fraud watch has not been declared... but the meteorologists are definitely keeping an eye on the radar. 👀

🚨 Upset of the Week

Sarrel Stuns Moltisanti's Mistress

Sarrel defeats Moltisanti's Mistress 501.63 – 399.93

Looking back on this matchup, I am still scratching my head... how the hell did Sarrel win this one?

Well, mostly blind luck.

Sarrel once again used zero waiver claims, yet somehow walked away with the biggest upset of the week as Moltisanti's Mistress completely forgot how to play fantasy baseball.

After looking like the hottest offense in the league for nearly a month, Moltisanti's lineup finally came crashing back to earth, managing only 260.1 points compared to Sarrel's modest 275.5.

Some of the biggest disappointments came from Shea Langeliers (15), Otto Lopez (16), and Bo Bichette (4). After carrying this team for weeks, the bats finally went ice cold.

The pitching staff did not exactly help him out either. Drew Rasmussen finished at -9, Cristopher Sánchez had by far his worst outing of the season at -19, and Ryan Feltner added another -14. It was simply one of those weeks where nothing went right.

One bad week does not erase what we've watched over the past month. While we have seen some inconsistent weeks, we are not worried about Moltisanti's Mistress whatsoever. Every great team lays an egg once in a while, and after the offensive tear this lineup has been on, this feels much more like an off week than the start of a collapse. If anything, next week's opponent should probably be the one who's nervous because this lineup is bound to pop off again.

On the Sarrel side, Mason Miller continues to be one of the most ridiculous relievers in fantasy baseball, somehow racking up 44.6 points in just three innings pitched. He also got another strong week from Kyle Schwarber (40) and Pete Crow-Armstrong (53).

But let's not pretend this suddenly means Sarrel has everything figured out. He still ignored the waiver wire, still left moves on the table, and still managed to stumble into a win because Moltisanti's Mistress chose the worst possible week for his entire roster to implode.

Somehow... someway... Sarrel walks away with the victory. The whole league is shocked, Moltisanti's Mistress is left wondering what the hell just happened, and fantasy baseball once again reminds us that sometimes it simply refuses to make sense.

🤏 Closest Matchup

Team Lill Survives Ice

Team Lill defeats Ice 757.4 – 738.67

This one hurts for Ice.

Ice put up the fourth-highest score of the week and still walked away with a loss. That is now two straight weeks of taking home the Closest Matchup Award (losing by just 10 points last week and only 18 this week), and he also claims this week's Most Points in a Loss award.

It has been a brutal stretch for a team that genuinely looked like it had turned the corner.

This matchup was a complete slugfest from the opening pitch on Monday until the final out on Sunday. The lead changed hands throughout the week, and heading into the final day it actually looked like Ice was going to pull it off... but just when it looked like he had finally caught a break, the trap door opened beneath him.

It was an uphill battle from the very beginning. Ice had to survive Cam Schlittler (79.13), the #4 player of the week, Paul Skenes (71.27), the #7 player, and Taj Bradley (69.3), the #9 player. That is an absolutely brutal trio to run into in the same matchup.

Ice certainly did his part. He rostered the #2 player of the week, Zack Wheeler (110.3), along with Dylan Cease (69.6), giving him more than enough firepower to beat most teams in the league.

One thing Ice will probably replay in his head is leaving Mike Trout's 14 points sitting on the bench. It would not have won him the matchup by itself, but one more small lineup decision on top of that could have changed everything.

In the end, this was one of those fantasy baseball matchups where both managers deserved to win. No matter who walked away victorious, the other was always going to be asking, "How unlucky can I possibly be?"

Unfortunately... this week, the answer was Ice.

🔮 Week 17 Preview:

🔥 Matchup of the Week

Bryce @ Moltisanti's Mistress

This is the clear matchup of the week.

Bryce enters riding a three-game winning streak and sits comfortably at 10–6, while Moltisanti's Mistress suddenly finds himself back at .500 (8–8) after last week's stunning upset loss to Sarrel.

Just one week ago Moltisanti was sitting atop the Power Rankings after looking like the hottest team in the league. One bad week doesn't erase that, but another loss suddenly puts him in an uncomfortable spot with the playoff pack closing in.

Bryce, meanwhile, has quietly silenced the fraud talk. Three straight wins and a 921-point explosion last week have him looking every bit like a legitimate contender again.

Two teams with very different momentum. One massive game with huge playoff implications.

VinSalv @ JohnnySack

JohnnySack has completed one of the best turnarounds of the season, climbing from dead last all the way to 9–7 and fourth place.

VinSalv, meanwhile, desperately needs a win to keep his playoff hopes alive. The talent is there, but the consistency has not been.

JohnnySack has looked like one of the league's best teams over the past two months. Another win would continue his remarkable climb up the standings.

Ice @ Justin

Justin finally reclaimed sole possession of first place, but nobody is confusing this team with the juggernaut we saw earlier this season.

The offense has cooled considerably, and while the wins are still coming, the dominant performances have disappeared.

Ice has been one of the unluckiest teams in the league over the past month. If he catches even one break this week, don't be shocked if he gives Justin everything he can handle.

Sarrel @ Wagner

Wagner has now dropped back-to-back games and has failed to crack 620 points for five straight weeks.

Fortunately for him, the schedule gives him what appears to be the perfect opportunity to get right against Sarrel.

Of course... we said the exact same thing about Moltisanti last week. Wagner cannot afford to overlook anybody right now.

SlammingMuff @ Team Lill

Team Lill climbed back to 8–8 after surviving Ice and remains firmly in the playoff hunt.

SlammingMuff's season is hanging by a thread. At 6–10, another loss would all but slam the door shut on any miracle comeback.

Team Lill has to take care of business. The margin for error is shrinking with every passing week.

Final Take:

Week 17 is all about momentum.

Bryce looks to continue his surge, Moltisanti's Mistress tries to bounce back, JohnnySack attempts to keep climbing, and both Justin and Wagner are searching for convincing wins after a month of underwhelming baseball.

The playoff picture is beginning to separate itself, and every matchup now feels like it could decide somebody's season.
