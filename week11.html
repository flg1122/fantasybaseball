🏆 Matchup of the Week Recap

Down Goes Justin

Wagner defeats Justin 838.17 – 651.87

Down goes Justin.

Let’s be real, after dropping back-to-back near-1000 point weeks, we all expected Justin not to have that same production again. Two weeks? Sure. Three weeks? That was unrealistic.

Wagner decimated Justin, and Justin declared the week over on Monday as per usual. This time, he was actually right.

On Monday alone, Wagner outscored Justin 116–12. Justin made a push later in the week, but he never truly recovered from getting punched in the mouth immediately.

Wagner owned him top to bottom. His hitters outscored Justin’s hitters 360-254, and his pitchers outscored Justin’s pitchers 478–397.

Wagner held the #2 overall player of the week in Noah Cameron, who dropped 103.5 points. The pitching avalanche did not stop there: Michael Soroka added 63, Roki Sasaki dropped 60, Bryce Elder put up 59, and Jacob Misiorowski chipped in 45.

Roki Sasaki being once rostered by Justin only makes this beatdown even funnier.

The piece that might haunt Justin the most is Riley Greene putting up 55 points against him after Justin traded him to Wagner for Logan Gilbert. Logan Gilbert then turned around and put up a dud against the Mets with only 14 points.

Justin has looked dominant, but Wagner might just be his kryptonite. Wagner has now handed Justin his only two losses of the season.

As they say in Call of Duty, the battle is lost, but the war has just begun. These two meet again in Week 20, and personally, I am hoping they face off when it matters most: in the playoffs.

📈 Win Streak Watch

JohnnySack Keeps Rolling

JohnnySack defeats Bryce 771.63 – 607.6

JohnnySack stays hot and extends his win streak to four straight games. What once looked like a lost season has suddenly turned into a legitimate playoff push.

Once again, JohnnySack was powered by a monstrous rotation. He got 78 from Sandy Alcantara, 61 from Shane Baz, 44 from Michael McGreevy, 44 from Carlos Rodón, and 37 from Nolan McLean.

The offense chipped in as well, led by Brice Turang, who went absolutely nuclear with 80 points, good for the #2 hitter of the week.

Although the final margin looks comfortable, Bryce did not go down easy. As late as Friday, projections actually favored Bryce to win this matchup.

Bryce was once again carried by Shohei Ohtani, who somehow topped himself with 104.5 points, finishing as the #1 player of the week for yet another week. He also got 54 from Logan Webb, a true innings eater that Bryce has to be thrilled to have back after being sidelined. Unfortunately for Bryce, the bloom has officially fallen off José Soriano's rose. After spending much of the first half looking like a league-winning steal, Soriano stumbled to a measly -1 point, giving Bryce exactly the opposite of the boost he needed.

But it was not enough.

Bryce is now back in free fall, while JohnnySack continues climbing. At some point, we have to ask the uncomfortable question: was Bryce's hot start just blind luck, or is this roster really just Shohei Ohtani and the power of friendship ?

📉 Bounce Back Win

Moltisanti's Mistress Takes Care of Business

Moltisanti's Mistress defeats VinSalv 580.07 – 276.03

Moltisanti's Mistress snaps his two-game losing streak and gets back into the win column.

On the other side, I am not entirely sure how a team goes from scoring 749 points last week to 276 points this week, but here we are.

VinSalv's team is basically the Yankees. When they're good, they look unbeatable. When they're bad, they look like a Double-A affiliate.

Moltisanti's Mistress was powered by Pete Alonso with 44, Otto Lopez with 53, Drew Rasmussen with 67, and Cristopher Sánchez with 54.

Rasmussen, in particular, continues to fly under the radar. He has quietly been one of the more reliable arms in the league all season.

Truthfully, there is not much else to take away from this matchup. VinSalv never really threatened, and Moltisanti's Mistress simply handled business.

It was not the most impressive score of the week, but a win is a win. After running into Team Lill and Justin in back-to-back weeks, Moltisanti's Mistress will gladly take it.

🥱 The Snooze Fest

SlammingMuff Handles Ice

SlammingMuff defeats Ice 502.13 – 394.3

This was supposed to be the golf course grudge match. Instead, it turned into two grandfathers fighting over the last recliner at the retirement home.

Both of these teams look completely asleep.

Ice left four waiver claims untouched, while Jon apparently did not realize he had a matchup until Sunday morning, when he burned a claim on Jack Flaherty of all people. Somehow, it worked out.

We will run through this snooze fest quickly so nobody falls asleep reading it.

SlammingMuff's lineup was awful, producing only 165 points compared to Ice's 244. Fortunately for Jon, Ice's rotation was somehow even worse, scoring only 150 points compared to SlammingMuff's 336.

SlammingMuff was carried by Jacob deGrom with 82 and Yoshinobu Yamamoto with 62.

It was also a great week for Jon to forget to set his lineup, as he left Shota Imanaga's -13 points safely on the bench.

Ice actually had the #5 hitter of the week in Jackson Chourio and still could not get it done.

At this point, both teams look like they are running on fumes. One won, one lost, but neither exactly inspired confidence.

😮 Survive and Advance

Team Lill Escapes Sarrel

Team Lill defeats Sarrel 625.83 – 560.8

This one was actually much closer than the final score suggests.

On Sunday, Sarrel held a narrow 483–481 lead and looked like he had a legitimate shot at pulling off the upset.

Which naturally leads to the question everyone is asking: what would have happened if Sarrel had actually used his waiver claims?

Once again, Sarrel left all four waiver claims untouched. At this point it is less of a strategy and more of a lifestyle.

Team Lill desperately needed this win after falling back to .500 following last week's loss to Wagner.

Team Lill must have been checking the Weekly Scoring Leaders page because he noticed Brandon Marsh was on an absolute heater. Marsh stayed hot this week, putting up 79.2 points, good for the #3 hitter of the week.

Lill also got strong production from Corbin Carroll with 53, Connor Prielipp with 69, and Adley Rutschman with 49.

On the Sarrel side, his hitters once again did their best to overcome managerial negligence. He got 80.3 from Pete Crow-Armstrong, the #1 hitter of the week, 44 from Bobby Witt Jr., and 54 from Miguel Vargas.

The frustrating part for Sarrel is that this was not some hopeless blowout. This game was right there for the taking. He left Hunter Goodman (73), Ernie Clement (40), and Gunnar Henderson (24) on his bench for a combined 138.7 points. That's a win right there for Sarrel had he not failed to set lineup.

Had Sarrel actually set his lineup and used his pickups, this could have been another unmitigated disaster for Team Lill.

Instead, Team Lill survives to see another day, climbs back above .500, and keeps himself firmly in the middle of the playoff race.

🔮 Week 11 Preview:

🔥 Matchup of the Week

Justin @ Wagner

It does not get bigger than this.

Two teams sit at 9–1. Two teams are riding massive win streaks. Two teams have spent most of the season battling for the top spot in the standings. And now they finally collide.

Justin looks like an absolute powerhouse right now, coming off back-to-back monster performances and looking every bit like the league's best team. Wagner, meanwhile, continues to survive despite injuries, questionable trades, and a roster that somehow refuses to die.

The winner takes sole possession of first place and becomes the first team in the league to reach 10 wins.

These are also the league's two highest-scoring teams. No excuses. No schedule merchant allegations. No luck arguments. Just two heavyweights stepping into the ring.

There is also a little history here.

Wagner handed Justin his only loss of the season back in Week 2. Since that defeat, Justin has not lost a single matchup and has ripped off an incredible 8-game winning streak.

Now Justin gets his chance at revenge. Was Week 2 the start of Wagner's dominance, or was it the spark that turned Justin into the league's most dangerous team?

One of these win streaks ends this week.

Bryce @ JohnnySack

JohnnySack enters Week 11 riding a 3-game winning streak and suddenly finds himself back in the playoff conversation.

Bryce finally got back into the win column last week, but it came against a struggling Sarrel squad. Now he faces a much tougher challenge against one of the hottest teams in the league.

JohnnySack would love to keep the streak rolling and gets a shot at a Bryce team that has looked shaky over the past few weeks while battling injuries throughout the roster.

Bryce still sits in third place at 6–4, but that cushion is shrinking fast. With multiple teams sitting at 5–5 and looking to make a move, another loss could make things very uncomfortable.

Perhaps the most shocking stat of all: JohnnySack has actually scored more points this season than Bryce despite sitting two games behind him in the standings.

Moltisanti's Mistress @ VinSalv

Moltisanti's Mistress enters this matchup riding a brutal 2-game losing streak.

Consecutive matchups against Team Lill and Justin have not done him any favors, and this feels like a prime opportunity to bounce back, reassert himself, and get back into the win column.

VinSalv, however, has quietly won two straight after taking down Ice and then absolutely dismantling SlammingMuff.

What looked like a dead season a few weeks ago suddenly has life. VinSalv has climbed back to .500 and finds himself right in the middle of the playoff race.

This one is sneaky good and could have major implications for the middle of the standings.

SlammingMuff @ Ice

Well, well, well.

The old golf course grudge match returns.

The all-time series between these two managers is tied 2–2, and SlammingMuff already took down Ice earlier this season.

SlammingMuff enters on a two-game losing streak and desperately needs a bounce-back performance. Meanwhile, Ice has now dropped five straight and is running out of time to save his season.

It always seems like struggling teams circle Ice on the schedule and think, "Yep, that's exactly what I need right now."

Of course, Ice probably feels the same way. If there was ever a team to get right against, it might be SlammingMuff as summer has just begun.

Because as league history has taught us, once the sun comes out, the boat hits the water, and the Silverado starts flying down the Parkway, SlammingMuff suddenly becomes vulnerable and occasionally forgets fantasy baseball exists.

Rivalry. Losing streaks. Desperation. Golf. What more could you ask for?

Team Lill @ Sarrel

Team Lill can finally take a deep breath.

After spending weeks battling contenders and suffering through one brutal matchup after another, Team Lill finds what should be a softer landing spot against Sarrel.

Sarrel is currently riding a 3-game losing streak and continues to look asleep at the wheel, but every losing streak eventually ends.

Team Lill needs a bounce-back win after another tough loss to Wagner. Sarrel needs proof that his season still has a pulse.

Will Team Lill finally catch a break, or will Sarrel wake up just in time to burst Team Lill's bubble?

Final Take:

Week 11 features the biggest matchup of the season so far as Justin and Wagner battle for sole possession of first place.

Meanwhile, Bryce and JohnnySack could shake up the playoff picture, Moltisanti's Mistress desperately needs a win, and the SlammingMuff-Ice rivalry gets another chapter.

The standings are starting to shake up, and every matchup now feels a little more important.
