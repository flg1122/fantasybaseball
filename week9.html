Week 9 Recap

Justin Breaks the League

Week 9 was chaotic but I will keep this recap short & sweet. Justin put up the highest score of the season thus far, Wagner survived another shaky week, JohnnySack keeps the comeback alive, and the Mid-Off somehow became the closest matchup.


🔥 Week 9 Snapshot

The League Keeps Getting Weirder

Week 9 gave us a little bit of everything: a historic scoring week, a true mid-off, another Wagner escape act, and JohnnySack suddenly looking alive after spending most of the season getting kicked in the teeth.

Justin finally shut the schedule merchant allegations up for at least one week, Team Lill picked up a statement win, and Bryce once again looked like Shohei Ohtani dragging around a collection of furniture.

🏆 Top Scorer of the Week

Justin Drops 1007.47

Justin was the top scorer of Week 9 with 1007.47 points.

This was not just the top score of the week. This was the top score of the entire season so far. Many people might assume this is a league record, but Team Lill still owns that crown after putting up 1137.74 in Week 17 last year.

Still, 1000+ points in a week is ridiculous. The schedule merchant allegations are not dead, but they have been temporarily silenced.

💀 Biggest Blowout

Justin Dumps On SlammingMuff

Justin defeats SlammingMuff 1007.47 – 605.9

This was the biggest blowout of the week, and it was not close. SlammingMuff came in hot after two huge wins, and Justin responded by putting him through a wood chipper.

This was supposed to be Justin’s first real challenge in weeks. Instead, he put up the best score of the season and made the league shut up for a few minutes.

🤏 Closest Matchup

The Mid-Off Delivers

VinSalv defeats Ice 588.57 – 558.03

Previously highlighted as the mid-off of the week, Ice vs VinSalv somehow became the closest matchup, with only a 30.54-point gap.

It was mid. It was ugly. It was exactly what we expected. But credit where credit is due: the mid-off gave us the tightest finish of Week 9.

🏆 Matchup of the Week

Team Lill Takes Down Moltisanti’s Mistress

Team Lill defeats Moltisanti’s Mistress 729.1 – 678.57

My matchup of the week was Moltisanti’s Mistress hosting Team Lill, and this one did not disappoint. Unless you were Moltisanti’s Mistress, of course.

This one was tight into Sunday before Team Lill pulled away in the final hours, putting up an impressive 124 points on Sunday — 84 from the lineup and 39 from the rotation.

Team Lill had the #1 player of the week, Ketel Marte, who dropped 104.8 points and helped seal the deal.

🍰 Schedule Merchant Watch

Justin Shuts Everybody Up

Justin defeats SlammingMuff 1007.47 – 605.9

I highlighted this as Justin’s first real challenge in weeks because he had been living on easy mode. He has since shut me up.

Putting up over 1000 points in a week is enough to silence everyone for a while. Justin was powered by Juan Soto with 74.3, the #2 hitter of the week, Kyle Harrison with 65.6, and Nick Kurtz with 67.2.

SlammingMuff had been riding momentum, but this week he ran into a buzzsaw.

📈 Win Streak Watch

JohnnySack Handles Sarrel

JohnnySack defeats Sarrel 602.63 – 391.53

JohnnySack’s lineup now feels awake. After starting the season painfully slow, back-to-back weeks of 300+ points from the lineup looks very promising.

JohnnySack was powered by 56 from Yandy Diaz, 36 from Rafael Devers, 46 from Kevin Gausman, and 52 from Joe Ryan.

Sarrel got blown out, but there were a few highlights: 49 from MacKenzie Gore and 48 from Ivan Herrera.

Sarrel drops to 2–7, while JohnnySack moves to 3–6. Suddenly, JohnnySack’s playoff push does not sound completely insane.

⚠️ Fraud Watch

Wagner Squeaks By Bryce

Wagner defeats Bryce 648.1 – 564.4

Wagner squeaks by another week, this time beating Bryce. Wagner has lost two of his best producers in the past two weeks: first Max Fried, and now Drake Baldwin goes down with an oblique strain. Not looking great for Wagner.

Although he was able to power through with a strong rotation, his lineup looked rough, putting up only 235 points. Sarrel, Ice, and VinSalv all had better lineup production than Wagner this week. That is disgusting.

Regardless, Wagner’s overachieving rotation carried him: 50 from Cade Cavalli, 59 from JT Ginn, and 53 from Cade Smith.

A quick check-in on Wagner’s trade acquisitions: Justin Wrobleski 5 points, Emmet Sheehan -7 points, and Austin Riley on the waiver wire. This shit is insane! Regardless, Wagner takes care of business & moves to an impressive 8-1.

As for Bryce, it was another week of the same story. This team still looks like Ohtani and nothing else.

🥱 The Mid-Off

VinSalv Survives Ice

VinSalv defeats Ice 588.57 – 558.03

Last week I highlighted this matchup as the mid-off, and it absolutely delivered. It looked like these two teams were trying to out-mid each other.

At this point, there is not much to say about these two teams. They stink.

Ice lost Vladimir Guerrero Jr. and Dylan Cease to injury this week, making a bad situation even worse. VinSalv probably does not even open the app anymore.

Sal Stewart stayed hot for VinSalv with 54.9 points. Ben Rice cooled off big time with a measly 10 points. Julio Rodriguez showed a pulse with 48.4, while Ronald Acuña Jr. disappeared with 7.8.

On the Ice side, he surprisingly got 51 from Luis Severino, 58 from Zac Gallen, and 49 from Mike Trout. And still lost.

🔮 Week 10 Preview:

Matchup of the Week:

Team Lill @ Wagner

This is my Matchup of the Week pick. Team Lill looks red hot after taking down Moltisanti’s Mistress and sliding into third place, overtaking Bryce by points.

Now Team Lill gets a chance to take down a very vulnerable Wagner team and spoil an 8-week win streak. Wagner keeps finding ways to survive, but the cracks are obvious. If Team Lill wants to prove he belongs near the top of the league, this is the matchup to do it.

Justin @ Moltisanti’s Mistress

Another great-looking matchup. Justin gets challenged by competitive teams in back-to-back weeks, fresh off putting up the highest score of the season.

Moltisanti’s Mistress is looking to get right after falling to Team Lill. If Moltisanti’s Mistress can slow Justin down, this could be one of the better matchups of the week. If not, Justin’s win streak might keep rolling.

JohnnySack @ Ice

JohnnySack looks to take advantage of yet another weak opponent as he continues his push toward a playoff spot. After back-to-back wins, this is exactly the type of matchup he needs to capitalize on.

Ice looks like his team is in shambles, and losing two of his best players does not help. If JohnnySack handles business here, the comeback story starts getting a little more real.

Sarrel @ Bryce

Sarrel has been struggling since defeating Moltisanti’s Mistress in Week 7, and Weeks 8 and 9 have not been kind to him. Now he gets a chance to take advantage of a beat-up, declining Bryce team.

On the other side, Bryce is riding a 3-game losing streak and desperately needs to right the ship. A loss here would make the fraud watch impossible to ignore.

VinSalv @ SlammingMuff

VinSalv just took down Ice, which does not say much, but he would love to keep the momentum going. Or maybe he would not. I am not fully convinced he even opens the app.

SlammingMuff is looking to get right after getting absolutely dumped on by Justin. On paper, this should be a bounce-back spot against an easy opponent. But in this league, managerial malpractice is always lurking.

Final Take:

Week 10 has real separation potential. Team Lill can shake up the top of the league, Justin can continue proving the doubters wrong, JohnnySack can keep his playoff push alive, and Bryce may be one more loss away from full-blown panic.
