🏆 Matchup of the Week Recap

📉 Win Streak Snapped

Moltisanti’s Mistress defeats JohnnySack 853.53 – 665.93

Down goes JohnnySack. All Good Things Must Come to an End!

After climbing from last place all the way into third place behind a six-game winning streak, JohnnySack finally saw his incredible run come to an end.

The writing was on the wall early. On Monday alone, Moltisanti's Mistress jumped out to a 163–85 lead. Tuesday only widened the gap, with another 125–97 advantage.

To JohnnySack's credit, he did not roll over. Friday nearly brought the comeback to life as he exploded for 225 points while Moltisanti's Mistress managed only 57. Unfortunately, the hole was simply too deep.

JohnnySack ran into one of the hottest lineups we've seen all season. Junior Caminero led the charge with an absurd 113 points, finishing as the #1 overall player of the week. He was joined by Pete Alonso (42), Otto Lopez (46), Wyatt Langford (48), and Bo Bichette (57).

The result was a complete offensive beatdown. Moltisanti's lineup outscored JohnnySack's 405–227.

Ironically, the pitching duel was about as even as it gets. Both staffs eclipsed 430 points, even with Brandon Woodruff delivering a monstrous 107-point performance for Moltisanti's Mistress. This matchup was decided almost entirely by the bats.

With the victory, Moltisanti's Mistress claws his way back to 7–7 and .500, while JohnnySack's magical run comes to an end as he also falls back to 7–7. The six-game streak is over, but after climbing from the league basement into the middle of the playoff race, JohnnySack has already proven this season is far from over.

🛑 Back on Track

Bryce Gets Right

Bryce defeats VinSalv 768.27 – 555.03

After slipping over the past few weeks from one of the league's top teams to just one game over .500, Bryce desperately needed a get-right matchup. Bryce got exactly that this week. There is nothing a struggling manager likes to see more than VinSalv on the schedule.

Bryce broke his foot off in VinSalv's ass, cruising to a 749–558 victory and improving to 8–6.

The difference was Bryce's rotation, which absolutely dominated with 518 pitching points. He was powered by Foster Griffin (105), Sonny Gray (90), Kumar Rocker (83), and Logan Webb (55).

Logan Webb continues to quietly be one of the most reliable pitchers in fantasy baseball. Over the month of June, he has posted an outstanding 0.71 ERA, and Bryce has to be thrilled to have his ace rounding into form at the right time.

Truthfully, VinSalv never really stood a chance. Bryce controlled this matchup from the start, got the bounce-back win he desperately needed, and moves to 8–6. VinSalv falls to 5–9 and continues searching for any sort of consistency.

🧊 Ice Is Melting the Narrative

Ice Makes It Three Straight

Ice defeats Sarrel 722.87 – 399.5

During our two-week hiatus, Ice has quietly been climbing out of the basement. Entering the week on a two-game winning streak, he made it three straight, improving to 5–9 after a bloody beatdown of Sarrel.

Despite sitting near the bottom of the standings, Ice's team has quietly gained a ton of momentum. Injured players are returning, the pitching staff has found its groove, and suddenly this roster looks far more dangerous than it did a month ago.

Ice's pitching staff once again led the way, piling up an incredible 477 pitching points. He got strong outings from Zack Wheeler (48), Dylan Cease (67), while Mookie Betts added 62 from the lineup.

Truthfully, Sarrel was never much of a challenge. But after the season Ice has endured, he'll gladly take the free win and continue building momentum.

As for Sarrel... what is there left to say?

Sarrel finished with just 70 innings pitched compared to Ice's 103. That is not bad luck—that is simply refusing to make waiver claims, failing to set your lineup, and continuing to leave injured players active.

At this point, Sarrel's biggest opponent isn't the rest of the league. It's himself. An absolutely fucking disgraceful excuse of season. He will surely be expunged next year.

🍀 Wagner Lucks Out AGAIN

Wagner Survives SlammingMuff

Wagner defeats SlammingMuff 533.8 – 512.03

Wagner escapes again - not because of skill, purely due to MANAGERIAL MALPRATICE ON JON'S END!

Three straight weeks without cracking 600 points, yet somehow he's won all three. At this point, Wagner might want to buy a lottery ticket because this run of fortune has been unbelievable.

His lineup has become downright detrimental during this stretch, producing a measly 182 offensive points this week. Somehow, it still didn't matter.

The biggest reason? SlammingMuff once again managed to beat himself. Jon left Andre Pallante (25.73) and Walker Buehler (20.37) sitting comfortably on his bench, leaving over 46 points unused.

Those two moves alone would have flipped the matchup and handed Wagner his second loss in three weeks. Instead, Wagner walks away with yet another escape.

At this point, Jon isn't just losing games—he's donating wins. What a fucking embarassment this team has been. If you're not going to set your lineup, why sign up for the league in the first place?

Wagner improves to 12–2, but once again, he leaves with a win that easily could have gone the other way.

🥊 Heavyweight Fight

Team Lill Outlasts Justin

Team Lill defeats Justin 849.27 – 774.53

What. A. Matchup.

This was an absolute heavyweight bout between two of the league's better teams, and it somehow lived up to the hype. Team Lill poured in 832 points, while Justin answered with 774. On most weeks, either score wins comfortably.

Team Lill's lineup was the difference, outscoring Justin's hitters 362–215. He was powered by Brandon Marsh (74), Jonathan Aranda (64), and Ketel Marte (54), who kept the offense rolling from top to bottom.

Justin's pitching staff, meanwhile, was absolutely ridiculous. His rotation piled up 558 points, easily outscoring Team Lill's 470. On most weeks, that alone wins you the matchup.

What's even crazier is Justin may have left history on the table. Despite scoring nearly 560 pitching points, he still absorbed 7 from Gerrit Cole, -29 from Shane Bieber, and -4 from Roki Sasaki. If even one or two of those outings go the other way, we could have been looking at one of the greatest pitching weeks this league has ever seen.

Team Lill wasn't perfect either. Paul Skenes and Cam Schlittler both cooled off, and Lill even left 35 points from Grant Holmes sitting on the bench. Fortunately for him, it never came back to haunt him.

When the dust settled, Team Lill walked away with one of the biggest wins of the season, improving to 7–7. Justin falls to 11–3, but after putting up 774 points, there's not much more you can ask from a team. Sometimes you simply run into a monster. And unfortunately for Justin, it seems like every time he losses it is a similar story.

🔮 Week 15 Preview:

🔥 Matchup of the Week

Wagner @ Moltisanti's Mistress

It does not get much bigger than this.

Wagner enters Week 15 sitting alone atop the league at 12–2, while Moltisanti's Mistress has battled all the way back to 7–7 after looking dead in the water just a few weeks ago.

Wagner has somehow continued stacking wins despite cooling off offensively in recent weeks, surviving several close calls that easily could have gone the other way. Meanwhile, Moltisanti's lineup has quietly become one of the hottest in the league, led by Junior Caminero's monster week and another strong offensive performance.

A Wagner victory keeps him in firm control of first place. A Moltisanti win not only pushes him above .500, but also opens the door for Justin to gain ground at the top of the standings.

One team is trying to defend first place. The other is trying to prove it belongs in the playoffs. This is the one to watch.

Justin @ JohnnySack

Justin has suddenly lost two of his last four games after looking nearly unbeatable for most of the season.

JohnnySack, meanwhile, saw his six-game winning streak come to an end last week, but still finds himself sitting at 7–7 and right in the middle of the playoff race.

Justin would love nothing more than to get back on track before Wagner creates too much separation at the top. JohnnySack would love an even bigger prize: knocking off one of the league's heavyweights while climbing above .500 for a third time this year.

This has upset potential written all over it.

VinSalv @ Team Lill

Team Lill clawed his way back to 7–7 after knocking off Justin in one of the best matchups of the season.

VinSalv, on the other hand, continues to be one of the league's biggest detriments. One week he looks unstoppable. The next, he struggles to reach 300 points.

Team Lill cannot afford a letdown after such a massive win. If he wants to stay in the playoff picture, this is the type of matchup he simply has to win.

VinSalv would love nothing more than to play spoiler.

Ice @ Bryce

Bryce finally appears to have stabilized after a rough stretch and now sits at 8–6.

Ice, meanwhile, has quietly become one of the hotter teams near the bottom of the standings, winning three straight after spending most of the season buried in the basement.

Bryce needs this one to keep pace in the playoff race. Ice has nothing to lose and has been playing much better baseball lately.

Do not be surprised if this one ends up closer than people expect.

Sarrel @ SlammingMuff

Two disgraceful teams with two disgraceful managers meet with plenty of pride on the line.

Sarrel enters at 3–11 after another frustrating week where unused waiver claims once again became part of the story. SlammingMuff sits at 5–9 and desperately needs a win to keep even the slightest playoff hopes alive.

Neither team has inspired much confidence lately, but somebody has to win.

The biggest question may simply be: who actually sets their lineup?

Final Take:

Week 15 features another massive showdown as Wagner looks to protect first place against a surging Moltisanti's Mistress squad.

Justin faces a dangerous JohnnySack team desperate to stay in the playoff hunt, while Team Lill looks to build on perhaps his biggest win of the season.

With several teams sitting at 7–7, nearly every matchup has playoff implications. The race is officially on.
