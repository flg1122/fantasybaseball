Week 8 Recap

The League Turns Upside Down

Bryce is in free fall, Wagner is showing cracks, Team Lill’s flaws got exposed, JohnnySack finally got back in the win column, and the middle of the league is officially a war zone.


🔥 League Chaos

Nobody Feels Safe

Week 8 completely flipped the league on its head. Bryce, who once looked like one of the best teams in the league, is suddenly falling apart. Wagner keeps winning, but the cracks are getting harder to ignore. Team Lill’s rotation still looks scary, but the lineup is starting to raise questions.

Meanwhile, JohnnySack finally stopped getting punched in the face by the schedule and immediately delivered the highest score of the week. SlammingMuff moved back into the mix, Moltisanti’s Mistress survived a huge matchup, and the league now has a 4–4 traffic jam that could shape the playoff race.

The top teams are still on top, but for the first time all season, everyone looks beatable.

🚨 Trade Market

The Riley Greene Saga

The trade market got stupid again this week. Three trades went down, and somehow Justin ended up walking into the market with Mitch Keller and walking out with Logan Gilbert.

Trade 1

Justin sends Roman Anthony to Bryce for Framber Valdez

Trade 2

Justin sends Mitch Keller to Ice for Riley Greene

Trade 3

Justin sends Riley Greene to Wagner for Logan Gilbert

I guess Bryce had seen enough from Framber Valdez. Fair enough. But the Riley Greene movement is absolutely mind numbing.

In his last 20 games, Greene is slashing .389 AVG, 3 HR, 8 RBI, and a 1.093 OPS. On the season, he is batting .320 with 4 homers, 23 RBIs, and an .883 OPS. That is not the kind of player managers usually pass around like a whore on her first day on campus. Riley Greene was just treated like a rental car.

Ice moving Greene when his team is already pitiful offensively is confusing enough. Justin immediately flipping him after robbing Ice is also confusing. Wagner paying Logan Gilbert to get him only adds another layer to the league-wide confusion.

The final result? Justin essentially flipped Mitch Keller into Logan Gilbert. Authorities are still investigating.

🏆 Matchup of the Week

Moltisanti’s Mistress Overpowers Bryce

Moltisanti’s Mistress defeats Bryce 580.33 - 498.13

As I said last week, Moltisanti’s Mistress going against Bryce was the matchup of the week, and it did not disappoint. On Saturday, Bryce was leading Moltisanti’s Mistress 351–347. Neck and neck. No room to breathe.

But in the end, Moltisanti overpowered Bryce and picked up a desperately needed win after falling to Sarrel last week. He was helped by 77 points from Cristopher Sanchez, the #8 player of the week, 52 from Yordan Alvarez, 40 from Emerson FootPenis, and 46 from Drew Rasmussen.

This was a massive bounce-back spot for Moltisanti's Mistress. After last week’s loss and the injury concerns piling up, this team needed to prove it could still beat a contender. Mission accomplished.

On the Bryce side of things, this team appears to be in a full-on free fall. A team that started the season hot, had all the momentum, and had plenty of shit to talk is now falling apart right before our eyes.

Last week, Bryce put up a measly 473 against SlammingMuff. This week, he put up 498 against Moltisanti's Mistress. Are we seeing this team regress to the norm? Is this who they are? Is Bryce a fraud?

Bryce did get 109 from Shohei Ohtani, good for the #1 player of the week. But outside of that, Bryce’s lineup was meek, uninspiring, and frankly looked like an easy matchup for any manager who can survive Shohei.

Managers are starting to realize Bryce’s team might just be Ohtani.

⚔️ Middle-Teams War

SlammingMuff Takes Down Team Lill

SlammingMuff defeats Team Lill 688.2-571.9

Another matchup I highlighted last week as one to watch was SlammingMuff hosting Team Lill. Both teams were coming off big wins. Both teams were trying to make a statement. SlammingMuff made his.

SlammingMuff got elite production from his starting lineup this week, with his hitters putting up 351 points compared to 258 from Team Lill’s hitters.

League sources are saying Team Lill has no intention of trading for hitters, despite his lineup’s weak performances over the past few weeks. He may want to reconsider.

The separation came on two key days. On Tuesday, SlammingMuff’s hitters put up 80 points while Lill’s put up 6. Then again on Friday, SlammingMuff’s hitters put up 70 while Lill’s put up 2. SlammingMuff comfortably won this matchup, and Friday is a key day to look back on.

SlammingMuff was powered by 68.5 points from Randy Arozarena, the #13 player this week, 61 from Teoscar Hernandez, the #17 player, and 60 from Peter Lambert.

On Team Lill’s side, the rotation still looks elite, although it is a lot less terrifying when Cam Schlittler does not get two starts. Schlittler gave him 43, still good, but nothing to turn heads.

Paul Skenes dropped 88 points, good for the #4 player of the week, which sounds fantastic until you realize he put up 71 on Tuesday and then a dud 17 on Sunday. Corbin Carroll also gave him 82 points, the #6 player of the week.

Definitely tough when you have two top-ten players on your squad and still cannot get the job done.

Lill also owned the worst player of the week in Jameson Taillon, who dropped -44.3 points, good for the #804 player of the week. Beautiful stuff.

SlammingMuff moves to 4–4, Team Lill drops to 4–4, and both teams are now stuck directly in the thick of it.

💥 Finally

JohnnySack Wins Again

JohnnySack defeats VinSalv 756.17 – 282.93

JohnnySack stops the bleeding, haults the 6 week losing streak, and finally gets his second win of the year. And this was not just a win. This was an absolute beatdown. He earns the title of highest scorer of the week, and now he just has to hope he did not waste all his points in a matchup where he did not need them.

On Saturday morning, JohnnySack was already blowing out VinSalv 409–147. This one was over early.

JohnnySack’s lineup finally woke up, racking up 326 points, powered by the awakening of Rafael Devers with 46, Jose Ramirez with 46, and Ryan Jeffers with 41.

Byron Buxton got off to a screaming hot start with 30 points by Wednesday on a few homers, but then was sidelined the rest of the week with an apparent hip injury. That would be a tough blow to this team if Buxton misses serious time.

JohnnySack’s pitching was lights out as well, putting up an impressive 429 points behind Casey Mize with 47, Kevin Gausman with 50, Joe Ryan with 45, and Jeffrey Springs with 41.

The bad news? JohnnySack’s rotation takes a huge blow losing Clay Holmes to a fractured fibula. Holmes had been JohnnySack’s second-best pitcher this year with 281 fantasy points, and now he could be sidelined until August or September.

There is not much to say about VinSalv’s team. His pitchers put up 79 total points combined, which Skenes and Wheeler can put up in a week alone. His lineup looked decent with 200 points, led by Ben Rice with 51 and Jazz Chisholm with 44, but this team is not moving anyone.

I am sure Vin did not set his lineup all week and just let the team ride. That will surely drop him in the softball batting order.

🍰 Schedule Merchant Watch

Justin Pisses All Over Ice

Justin defeats Ice 732.07 – 655.9

Justin pissed all over Ice in another cakewalk matchup. Let’s just call it what it is: Ice’s team looks terrible, and every manager looks forward to playing him.

Make it three straight weeks of losing for Ice with a mediocre roster. Justin, however, made a statement with 732 points, second best on the week. He may be called the schedule merchant, but he reminded the league how he got to his impressive record.

Their hitters put up eerily similar numbers, with Justin’s lineup scoring 287 and Ice’s scoring 289. The difference was pitching: 444 for Justin compared to 366 for Ice.

Ice made some bizarre waiver wire moves this week. Monday, he dropped Jacob Wilson for Spencer Jones. Tuesday, he dropped Spencer Jones for Luis Arraez. Then again Tuesday, he dropped Jorge Soler for Brooks Lee.

Many people tried explaining to Ice that you want to spend your waiver claims on pitching, but he thought he was outsmarting everybody, so we will let him be. Regardless, that is three mind-boggling waiver moves in a week. Ice probably had a shot at winning if he just grabbed four pitchers instead.

Justin was powered by 71 from Nick Kurtz, the #11 player of the week, 55 from the KING OF NEW YORK Juan Soto, and straight-up consistency from his rotation: Bradish 46, Suarez 42, Valdez 41, and Sale 41.

He also got a great contribution from Jhoan Duran with 26 points, who he absolutely stole from Wagner.

Ice had the #3 player of the week in Zack Wheeler, who put up 98 points over two starts with 14.1 IP and 12 Ks. He also got 68 from Bailey Ober, the #14 player of the week, and absolutely wasted it. Additionally, he picked up Luis Arraez mid-week who got him 61 and put 77.8 to solidify himself as the #7 player of the wek. Despite all of that, Ice still couldn't get it done.

What a freaking disgrace. MANAGERIAL MALPRACTICE.

⚠️ Wagner's Cracks are Showing

Wagner Squeaks By Sarrel

Wagner defeats Sarrel 629.13 – 567.3

Wagner squeaks by Sarrel to win 629.13–567.3. Saturday, this looked close, then Wagner eventually ran away with it.

We are really starting to see the impact that trade had on Wagner’s team. Barely beating Sarrel is not something you expect from the top-ranked team at this point in the year.

Sarrel got more elite production from Dave Kingman, also known as Kyle Schwarber, who put up 67 points, the #15 player of the week. He also got 49 from Bobby Witt and 43 from Bryce Harper.

Sarrel actually outscored Wagner’s hitters 283 to 274, but Wagner made up for it on the mound. Sarrel has been encouraged all year to pick up starters, but he just does not learn. Sarrel’s starters put up 265 compared to 354 from Wagner’s.

This week, Wagner’s team also lost Max Fried, the top scorer on his roster. Fried hits the 15-day IL with an elbow injury, and that type of issue can easily linger.

Wagner got 33 from trade acquisition Justin Wrobleski, who got humbled in a start against the Angels of all teams. He also got 82 from Michael Soroka, the #5 player of the week, and 61 from Jacob Misiorowski.

Sarrel easily could have won this one and humbled Wagner’s overrated team. But he just could not get it done.

🔮 Week 9 Preview:

Matchup of the Week:

Team Lill @ Moltisanti’s Mistress

This is my Matchup of the Week pick. Both teams sit at 4–4, and both teams are trying to climb above .500. Moltisanti’s Mistress held his own against Bryce this week and picked up a huge win. Now we get to see if Team Lill’s pitching staff can shut him down.

Team Lill’s rotation still looks dangerous, but the lineup has been questionable. Moltisanti’s Mistress has injuries to worry about, but the production is still there. Winner starts looking like a serious playoff threat. Loser gets dragged right back into the middle-class war.

SlammingMuff @ Justin

This might be the first real challenge Justin has had in a few weeks. SlammingMuff is coming off two huge wins and is now trying to ride that momentum into a three-game win streak.

Justin still sits near the top of the league, but the schedule merchant allegations have not gone away. If SlammingMuff takes him down, the league will have plenty to say. This one is absolutely worth watching.

Ice @ VinSalv

MID vs MID. Both teams stink. Both teams need wins. This will surely be a snooze fest, but if VinSalv actually sets his lineup, he probably comes away with this one.

Bryce @ Wagner

Fraud 1 vs Fraud 2. Bryce desperately needs a win here as he rides a two-week losing streak. What better way to prove the doubters wrong than taking down the team sitting in first place — a position Bryce once confidently held.

Wagner keeps winning, but the cracks are showing. Bryce keeps losing, and the fraud watch is getting louder. This matchup could either stop Bryce’s free fall or push him even deeper into panic mode.

Sarrel @ JohnnySack

JohnnySack just got back into the win column this week, and now he gets what should be a nice matchup to keep the win streak alive.

If JohnnySack can pick up this win and move to 3–6, he will start to feel a lot better about making a push toward the playoffs. Sarrel has been more competitive lately, but this is a matchup JohnnySack needs to win.

Final Take:

Week 8 changed the entire feel of the league. Bryce is sliding, Wagner looks vulnerable, the 4–4 teams are stacking up, and JohnnySack finally got off the mat.

Between Bryce collapsing, JohnnySack finally winning, and the Yankees dropping the Subwary Series to the Mets, this may have been the most enjoyable week of the season yet for certain members of the league.
