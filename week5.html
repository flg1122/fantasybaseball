📰 Week 5 Recap

Week 5: Johnny Sack Implodes, Justin Stops Crying, Wagner Keeps Rolling

Week 5 delivered a lot — dominant performances, complete collapses, and the emergence of a quiet contender nobody seems to be talking about yet.

Some teams are separating. Others are falling apart. Let’s get into it.

👑 Justin Can Stop Crying, Handles Team Lill

All week long, Justin cried.

“This is a loss.”
“No chance I win.”
“Schedule screwed me.”

Final score: 780–618 win over Team Lill.

Alright. You can shut up now.

Justin improves to 4–1, powered by:

  • Seiya Suzuki — 80 pts
  • Nick Kurtz — 63 pts
  • Strong depth across the board

Meanwhile, Team Lill didn’t exactly roll over:

  • Cal Raleigh — 74 pts
  • Josh Naylor — 60 pts

But it wasn’t enough.

Justin bitched and moaned all week… then won comfortably.

Classic.

🧨 Johnny Sack Implodes… Again

Another week, another unmitigated disaster for Johnny Sack — now sitting at 1–4 after falling to Moltisanti’s Mistress 619–532.

And this one hurts.

Despite an absolutely dominant 76-point outing from Nolan McLean, it didn’t matter. Completely wasted.

Just like the Mets would.

What a waste.

The real issue? Pitching. Again.

Johnny’s staff was nothing short of catastrophic:

  • Jack Flaherty: -31
  • Merrill Kelly: -29
  • Joe Ryan: disappointing
  • Christian Scott: questionable add

At some point you have to ask:

Is Johnny Sack trying to get too smart?

No catcher. Over-managing the roster. Chasing upside instead of stability.

Whatever the plan is — it’s not working.

⚖️ Ice Handles Business

Ice picks up a much-needed win over Sarrel, 626–545, improving to 2–3.

Nothing crazy. Nothing flashy. Just solid production:

  • Riley Greene — 80 pts
  • Alex Bregman — 57 pts
  • Vladimir Guerrero Jr. — 43 pts

Meanwhile, Sarrel drops to 0–5, still searching for that first win.

At this point, Sarrel needs to try something else.

🕶️ Wagner Keeps Rolling

Wagner moves to 4–1 after taking down SlammingMuff, 539–457.

And it’s time to say it clearly:

Wagner is a problem.

No noise. Just wins.

Even with:

  • Max Muncy — 78 pts for Slamming
  • 6 pitchers going Sunday

…SlammingMuff still couldn’t get it done.

Wagner is quietly stacking wins while everyone else is focused elsewhere.

That won’t last much longer.

💀 Bryce Destroys VinSalv

This one wasn’t close.

Bryce wins 689–386, moving to 4–1.

Let’s just be honest:

It’s not hard to beat someone who scores 386.

Bryce was led by:

  • Shohei Ohtani — 93 pts

Meanwhile, VinSalv drops to 2–3 and has a serious issue:

Pitching.

Vin’s entire pitching staff combined for 74 points this week.

Nolan McLean put up 76 alone.

That’s a problem.

🚨 Free Agent Alert

Two of the biggest performers this week?

Undrafted.

  • Chase Dollander — 139 pts (Top scorer of the week)
  • Spencer Torkelson — 92 pts

Let that sink in.

139 points… on waivers.

Maybe if someone appreciated the comish's hard work they could've reaped the benefits.

Somebody paying attention…

…could’ve had that.

Injured.
